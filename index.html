My Story

I was born in Bomso and lived there until 2013, when my family moved to Trede. I currently live there with my parents and my two siblings. My roots also trace back to Kwahu Obo in the Eastern Region of Ghana. From a young age, I was the person everyone turned to when things were broken. I had a natural confidence in fixing software issues, often figuring them out as I went along.

In 2022, I graduated from Opoku Ware School as a Science student. While my heart was set on medicine or anesthesia, I chose Nursing at Mampong Nursing and Midwifery Training College. It was a choice recommended by my grandmother, who is one of the best nurses I know. Nursing has transformed me; I went from someone who struggled to provide emotional support to someone who finds deep fulfillment in connecting with patients.

My drive to be a spokesperson lead me to serve as the SRC President. I have dedicated myself to ensuring no student is left behind due to academic or administrative hurdles. Outside the clinic, I am a digital explorer, gaming and creating content, working toward a future where I can heal with my hands and build with my mind.
